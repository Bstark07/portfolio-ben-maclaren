In the early days of mainstream 3D Printing, I ran educational workshops as part of the ANU Maker Club and consulted on 3D Printing systems for business as part of my own business called 3DForge.

I had accumulated a wealth of information and experience regarding 3D Printing and teaching, Over the course of a month I setout and completed: "A Short Illustrated Guide to 3D Printing".

{%include gallery style="center"%}

The main goal of this guide was:

  • Content easy to understand, comprehensive yet short
  • Useful to both newcomers to 3D Printing and seasoned users
  • Contain real world examples small and large
  • Provide links and information for further learning
  • Cater to audiences as young as high-school age.

The final result is a 41 page visually pleasing guide freely available to anyone and satisfying all 5 of the desired goals.

It has been distributed by a few local teachers to students and used by the local community.
